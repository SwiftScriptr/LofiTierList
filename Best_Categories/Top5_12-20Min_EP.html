The following albums bring consistent joy

In this List, I struggled to give true representation to the best of many artists work; therefore there will surely be an overflow outside the Top5.
This segment is where majority of classic lofi resides IMO; as this timespan is a sweetspot of creativity and restrain for most composers;
Not too long to collect the scraps of ideas to fill half an hour, and not too short for the listener to ask for more.
Anyone who cannot produce respectable EP's within this cat doesn't deseve to be listened to (a personal belief of mine).

  1. Ian Ewing - Almost Home

    2. This tops the List due to it being a personal favorite of mine; it follows the format of a story told like a shortfilm/music video.
    The following tracks are top quality and switch between the three genres Jazz, hipHop and funk, having splices of techno and synths.

  2. Junior State - Levitate

    3. This has the embodiment of victory throughout the whole playlist, this is uplifting and encouraging so it had to be a close second on the list.
    I loved the way how the major chords where used differently and sounded like it was a production instead of plain monotone music.

  3. Arbour - Old Growth

    4. Three words describe the attributes of this album; grungy, dynamic and funky. When first listening to this I didn't know what to expect (a little mature at the end),
    and so neither will you as it truly has a 'controlled chaos' so to speak. There is quality and a uniqueness that seperates this from others so it is an aquired taste.

  4. Ruck P - Destination

    5. Is the most cohesive album of the two that he has done telling a story that ended up being more production based completing the story told.
    The consistent use of vocals reflect the output and add to the emotion of the story better than his next album.

  5. Sw√òrn - Reflection

    6. This was really Dynamic and I was glad it could make the top 5. It had more than 60% of great tunes and the implemented sounds used to create the tracks deserved it's own space.

  6. Joint 6th place

    7. No Spirit - Snacks: Of the two ablbums I have, this one better portrays the slow-fi chillhop genre. A clean smooth and dynamic EP allowing relaxation and multitasking.

    Evil Needle - Lost Tape: This is a favourite of mine but it does come with some complications, it is dynamic funky and a great piece, bbut needs headphone to be best experienced.
    It isn't a BlueWednesday but without headphones there can be a flat sensation, turning up the volume too far pops the bass with static.

    Chillhop Beat Tapes Teddy Roxpin: Lastly, this beat tape is a solid treat; it is unique and playfull with the Dj vibes it produces.

  7. The seventh Selection

    8. Plusma & Guillaume Muschalle - Parasol: I appreciate the way this EP shows true Jazziness show casing brass instruments, which I used to play alot.
    There's an appreciation of purely playable sounds weighing in at 80% whilst the the synthetics are 20%, all chords are bright majors and it's really a nostalgic goodtime record.

    Blue Wednesday - The Great Escape: This is his most diverse album, which is saying something as mostly he's a pianoholic. there's more bass and pitch changes in the keys used.
    As the name suggests it is an ep cohesive enough to display a story which is just gratifiable for a change.

    Xander - Found Memories: There are tracks here on their own worth their salt, however, after a while, even when you listen periodically; you (well I) may find it repetetive.
    There are chillhop staples in here better displayed as a refreshing uplifting burst of nostalgia on the livestreams and radio, but not the album as they become predictable.

That's all for now folks, if you appreciate the content, share this space and reccommend it to the people you know who will appreciate the change of speed in the worklife and freetime. There may be more to come.
